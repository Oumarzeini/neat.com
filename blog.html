10 Ways to Organise Your Day with Digital Notes Whether you're a student, a professional, or just someone trying to keep your life on track, how you organise your day can make or break your productivity. Here's how digital notes can become your secret weapon:
1- Start With a Daily Plan Open your note-taking app first thing in the morning and jot down a to-do list. Break it into Must Do, Should Do, and Nice to Do categories.
2- Use Tags or Labels Organise your notes with tags like #urgent, #home, or #college. That way, you can filter tasks fast and stay focused.
3- Time-Block Your Schedule Dedicate specific time blocks for each task in your notes. Write it like: 10:00 - 11:00: Study for law exam.
4- Pin Your Priority Note Use the app's pin or favourite feature to keep your main plan for the day easily accessible.
5- Quick Notes Section Always have a section for jotting ideas or reminders on the fly‚Äîlike a digital sticky note.
6- End-of-Day Recap Before bed, review what you did and write a short reflection: What went well? What needs improving?
7- Link Notes Together Connect related notes (e.g., your reading list with your study schedule). It reduces search time and helps with focus.
8- Voice Notes When You're Busy Too busy to type? Use the voice note feature for on-the-go thoughts.
9- Weekly Planning Note Every Sunday, create a ‚ÄúThis Week‚Äù note where you lay out your focus areas.
10- Declutter Every Friday Review your notes weekly. Archive old ones, delete what's no longer relevant, and keep your digital space tidy.

üìù The Cornell Method - Structured and Study-Ready What It Is: A method designed to help students actively review and retain material.
How It Works: Divide your note page into 3 parts: Cue, Notes, and Summary.
Write detailed notes during a lecture or reading in the Notes section.
After class, fill in the Cue column with questions or prompts based on the notes.
At the bottom, summarise the topic in a few lines.
Best For: Students, exam revision, lectures, or structured study sessions.
Why It Works: It encourages active recall and forces you to engage with the material after taking it in.
üß© Zettelkasten - The Idea Network What It Is: A more creative, long-term knowledge system used by writers, researchers, and thinkers.
How It Works: Break ideas into atomic notes (just one idea per note).
Assign each note an ID and tag or link it to related notes.
Over time, your notes form a web of connected knowledge.


Best For: Long-term projects, deep learning, essay writing, creative work.
Why It Works: You build a second brain‚Äîa connected, searchable system that grows with you.
